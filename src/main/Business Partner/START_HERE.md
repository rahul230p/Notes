## 🎤 The 45-Minute Interview Breakdown

```
0-5 min:    Warm-up, intro
5-10 min:   They describe a scenario (listen carefully)
10-15 min:  YOU ASK CLARIFYING QUESTIONS (your turn)
15-25 min:  You propose approach (explain your thinking)
25-35 min:  Discussion, iteration, feedback
35-40 min:  "Tell me about a project" story
40-45 min:  Wrap-up, any final questions
```

## 🎯 Success Criteria

You'll know you're ready when you can:

- ☐ Ask 3-4 clarifying questions without thinking
- ☐ Explain data schemas to someone with no technical background
- ☐ Describe a project where you worked cross-functionally
- ☐ List trade-offs for any data solution
- ☐ Connect data work to business impact
- ☐ Handle "I don't know" gracefully ("Here's how I'd figure it out")
- ☐ Show flexibility and iteration mindset
- ☐ Explain your thinking out loud clearly

## 🚨 Common Mistakes (Avoid These)

❌ Jump to solution without questions  
❌ Use technical jargon unnecessarily  
❌ Only think about technical elegance  
❌ Don't mention other teams/collaboration  
❌ Ignore business context  
❌ Be rigid ("my way is best")  
❌ Not prepare your project story  
❌ Talk too much, listen too little
