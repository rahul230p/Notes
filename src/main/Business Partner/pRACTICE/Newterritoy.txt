🎯 SCENARIO: DoorDash Expanding into a New City (Charlotte, NC)

"DoorDash is expanding into Charlotte, NC - a mid-sized US market. How would you
approach launching? What metrics would you track? How would you decide whether to scale?"

================================================================================

🎯 OPENING (Say this first)

"I'd approach this as a PHASED MARKETPLACE LAUNCH, because success in a new city
evolves over time. Rather than a big bang launch, I'd use three phases with
clear go/no-go criteria for each."

================================================================================

🧠 PHASE 1: LAUNCH READINESS (First 4–6 Weeks)

GOAL: "Validate that the marketplace can function reliably."

CLARIFY ASSUMPTIONS (Say this explicitly):
- Mid-sized US city (Charlotte: 1M+ population)
- Similar density to Raleigh/Nashville
- Limited initial coverage area (5-10 ZIP codes)
- Target demographic: Urban professionals
- Competitive context: Uber Eats, Grubhub already present

---

CUSTOMERS (Demand Side)

KPIs:
- DAU: 10k–20k (realistic for phase 1)
- First → Second Order Conversion: >30% (do they trust us?)
- D7 Retention: >25% (coming back within 7 days?)
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): ≤ $30 (sustainable?)
- AOV: $25-35 (healthy order size?)

Why this works:
- Early repeat usage > raw DAU
- Repeat rate shows actual product-market fit
- If 30% conversion to 2nd order, we have a product people like

GO/NO-GO GATE:
- IF: D7 retention >25% AND conversion >30% → PROCEED to Phase 2 ✅
- IF: D7 retention <15% OR conversion <20% → PAUSE, investigate why
- IF: CAC >$50 → STOP, market too expensive

---

DASHERS (Supply Side)

KPIs:
- Dasher Utilization: 70–80% (healthy balance)
- Avg Delivery Time: <30 min target (Charlotte baseline)
- Orders per Dasher/Hour: 3-4 (reasonable productivity)
- Acceptance Rate: >75% (drivers willing to accept)
- Weekly Active Dashers: Enough to cover peak +20% buffer

Guardrail:
- Avoid over-utilization early
- If >85% = not enough supply = long waits = churn
- Better 80% with happy dashers than 95% with angry ones

GO/NO-GO GATE:
- IF: Utilization 70-80% AND acceptance >75% → Good balance
- IF: Utilization <60% → Too many dashers, reduce incentives
- IF: Utilization >85% AND delivery time >40 min → Supply shortage

---

MERCHANTS (Supply Side)

KPIs:
- Order Fulfillment Rate: ≥95% (are merchants taking orders?)
- Avg Orders/Merchant/Day: 5-15 (healthy volume)
- Merchant Onboarding Velocity: 30-50 merchants/week
- Avg Order Accuracy: ≥96% (getting orders right)
- Merchant Churn: <5%/month (are they happy?)

Why this matters:
- If merchants can't fulfill → complaints → churn

GO/NO-GO GATE:
- IF: Fulfillment ≥95% AND churn <5% → Stable, good selection
- IF: Fulfillment <90% → Too busy, reduce acquisition
- IF: Only 20 merchants → Not enough variety

---

✅ PHASE 1 SUCCESS CRITERIA (All three must be true):

- ✓ Customers: D7 retention >25%, conversion >30%
- ✓ Dashers: Utilization 70-80%, delivery time <30 min
- ✓ Merchants: Fulfillment ≥95%, churn <5%

If all three → Proceed to Phase 2
If any one fails → Fix that side first

================================================================================

🧠 PHASE 2: EARLY TRACTION (Months 2–3)

GOAL: "Confirm users find value and come back. Expand coverage and merchants."

EXPANSION PLAN:
- Geographic: 5-10 → 15-20 ZIP codes
- Merchants: 50+ merchants (build variety)
- Dashers: 200+ active dashers
- Timeline: 4-8 weeks

NORTH-STAR METRIC ⭐

"Orders per MAU" (Orders per Monthly Active User)
- Shows: How much is each user using DoorDash?
- Better than DAU (avoids one-time users)
- Target: 8-12 orders/MAU/month (2-3 per week)
- Why: If MAU growing but orders/MAU declining = retention problem

SUPPORTING KPIs:

- DAU Growth: +10-15% WoW (sustainable, not promo-driven)
- Order Frequency: Orders per active user (increasing month-over-month)
- D30 Retention: >40% (still using after 30 days?)
- Repeat Rate: >50% (half of users coming back)
- ARPU: Increasing or stable

DASHER SIDE - Phase 2:

- Supply/Demand Ratio: Maintain 70-80% utilization as we scale
- Dasher Earnings: Consistent (~$15-18/hour in Charlotte)
- If adding users but not orders → earnings drop → dasher churn
- Dasher Retention: >60% week-over-week

MERCHANT SIDE - Phase 2:

- Merchant Variety: 50+ merchants across categories
- Revenue/Merchant: Should increase (they earn more as orders increase)
- Merchant NPS: >40 (are they happy?)
- Top 10 Merchants: Shouldn't exceed 30-40% of orders (concentration risk)

🎯 PHASE 2 SUCCESS CRITERIA:

- ✓ Orders per MAU: 8-12 orders/month
- ✓ D30 Retention: >40%
- ✓ Dasher Utilization: Still 70-80% (not degraded)
- ✓ Merchant Revenue: Increasing

→ If yes, proceed to Phase 3

================================================================================

🧠 PHASE 3: SUSTAINABLE SCALE (Months 3+)

GOAL: "Validate unit economics before scaling nationally."

BUSINESS KPIs (Profitability):

- Revenue/Order: $5-7/order (delivery fee + commission + ads)
- Cost/Order: <$4 (dasher pay + platform + support + payment processing)
- Contribution Margin: 20-30%+ (revenue - variable costs)
- Customer Incentives: Declining as % of revenue
  - Phase 1: 20% of revenue (heavy discounts for trial)
  - Phase 3: <10% of revenue (customers convinced)
- Marketplace Margin: 15%+ before fixed costs

MERCHANT ECONOMICS:

- Revenue/Merchant/Month: $500-1000 (healthy?)
- Take Rate: 25-30% (what % of food sales we take)
- Merchant LTV: How long do they stay?
- Merchant CAC: How much to acquire?
- Merchant Payback: <6 months

DASHER ECONOMICS:

- Utilization: 70-80% (stable)
- Earnings: $15-18/hour (competitive for Charlotte)
- Supply/Demand: Balanced (not oversaturated)
- Retention: >60% week-over-week

🎯 PHASE 3 DECISION GATE (All four must be yes):

- ✓ Unit Economics: Positive contribution margin
- ✓ Sustainable CAC: Payback within 6 months
- ✓ Market Penetration: 15-20% of addressable market
- ✓ All Three Sides: Customers happy, Dashers earning, Merchants profitable

→ If yes: Scale to next 5-10 cities ✅
→ If no: Optimize Charlotte further

================================================================================

🚨 RISK ANALYSIS - What Could Go Wrong?

RISK 1: Customer Acquisition Too Expensive
- Problem: CAC >$50, hard to payback
- Warning: DAU not growing despite high promo spend
- Fix: Reduce promo, switch to organic
- Escalate: If CAC >$60 for 2+ weeks, pause acquisition

RISK 2: Supply Shortage (Not Enough Dashers)
- Problem: Utilization >85%, delivery time >40 min
- Customer Impact: Longer waits → Lower conversion → Churn
- Fix: Increase dasher incentives, targeted recruitment
- Escalate: If delivery time >45 min for 3+ days, pause acquisition

RISK 3: Merchant Fulfillment Issues
- Problem: Fulfillment rate <90%, orders getting cancelled
- Customer Impact: Try to order → Cancelled → Frustrated
- Fix: Better training, smaller batches, manage surge
- Escalate: If fulfillment <85%, focus on merchant ops

RISK 4: Low Retention (Customers Not Returning)
- Problem: D7 <15%, D30 <25%
- Investigation: Long times? Bad accuracy? High prices? No variety?
- Fix: Root cause analysis
- Escalate: If retention collapsing, don't scale

RISK 5: Competitive Pressure
- Problem: Uber Eats, Grubhub established, lower prices
- Can't win on price forever
- Fix: Differentiate on speed, variety, experience
- Escalate: Monitor weekly market share

================================================================================

📊 STAKEHOLDER COMMUNICATION

Product Team:
- DAU, retention, D7, conversion
- Churn rate, NPS
- Question: "Are customers happy?"

Operations Team:
- Dasher utilization, delivery time
- Merchant fulfillment, orders/merchant
- Question: "Is marketplace balanced?"

Finance Team:
- Revenue/order, cost/order, margin
- CAC, payback period, unit econ
- Question: "Are we profitable?"

Executive Leadership:
- Orders per MAU (overall momentum)
- Marketplace health (all three sides working?)
- Go/no-go decision (scale or pause?)

🗓️ WEEKLY CADENCE:

Monday: Review Phase Gates → Any emerging issues?
Wednesday: Partner Standups → All teams aligned?
Friday: Decision Point → Keep plan or adjust?
End of Phase: Final go/no-go for next phase

3️⃣ CLEAN 2-MINUTE ANSWER (Interview-Perfect)

"I'd approach launching Charlotte as a PHASED MARKETPLACE ROLLOUT with clear
go/no-go criteria at each stage.

PHASE 1 (4-6 weeks): OPERATIONAL READINESS
My goal is to validate that the marketplace functions reliably. I'd focus on
three sides:
- Customers: Track DAU, repeat rate, and D7 retention >25%
- Dashers: Ensure utilization stays healthy at 70-80%, delivery times stable
- Merchants: Confirm 95%+ fulfillment and healthy order volume

If those metrics are good, we proceed.

PHASE 2 (2-3 months): EARLY TRACTION
I'd expand coverage and merchant count, using ORDERS PER MAU as the north-star.
This shows whether each user finds value and comes back.

PHASE 3 (Months 3+): SUSTAINABLE SCALE
Before scaling further, I'd validate unit economics—revenue per order must
exceed variable costs. I want positive contribution margin and CAC payback
within 6 months.

As a data engineer, my job is ensuring these metrics are clearly defined,
consistently measured, and available to leadership for go/no-go decisions.
I'd set up monitoring for risks: if delivery time spikes, if retention drops,
if merchant fulfillment falters."
