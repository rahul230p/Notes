## 🧠 Mental Models to Master

### The Diagnostic Tree (for "why did X drop?" questions)
```
Problem Detected
    ├─ Is it DEMAND? (traffic, conversion, AOV)
    ├─ Is it SUPPLY? (availability, acceptance rate)
    ├─ Is it QUALITY? (ratings, delivery time)
    ├─ Is it OPERATIONS? (bugs, system issues)
    └─ Is it EXTERNAL? (competition, market, weather)
```

### Metric Hierarchy (what to prioritize)
```
TIER 1: Fundamental (GMV, Orders, MAU, Growth)
TIER 2: Engagement (Repeat rate, Retention, Frequency)
TIER 3: Quality (NPS, Rating, Delivery time)
TIER 4: Economics (CAC, LTV, Margin)
TIER 5: Operations (Supply, Performance, Consistency)
```

### Unit Economics (always know this)
```
CAC (cost to get customer) + LTV (value they generate) = Profitable?

Healthy: LTV:CAC > 3:1
Good: LTV:CAC > 5:1
Struggling: LTV:CAC < 3:1
```

## ✅ Pre-Interview Checklist

- ☐ I understand why clarifying questions matter (go first, not last)
- ☐ I can name 5 types of business partner questions
- ☐ I can use a template to answer most questions
- ☐ I know the metric hierarchy and can explain interconnections
- ☐ I can structure a root cause analysis
- ☐ I can design a simple A/B test
- ☐ I can identify trade-offs
- ☐ I can connect metrics back to business impact
- ☐ I can think about multiple stakeholders
- ☐ I can explain why "correlation ≠ causation"

---

## 🚨 Things NOT to Do

- ❌ Skip clarifying questions
- ❌ Propose solutions before understanding problem
- ❌ Ignore trade-offs
- ❌ Only think about one stakeholder
- ❌ Assume instead of confirming
- ❌ Jump to overly complex solutions
- ❌ Forget the "so what?" (business impact)
- ❌ Talk at them instead of with them
- ❌ Over-explain simple things
- ❌ Be unable to adapt when redirected

---

## 💡 Your Secret Weapon

**Ask clarifying questions first.**

3-4 well-placed questions at the start will:
- Show you think deeply
- Prevent wasted time on wrong problem
- Set up the rest of your answer
- Keep interviewer engaged

Most candidates skip this. You won't.

1. **Clarifying questions** - What are the 3-4 I'd ask?
2. **Metric hierarchy** - What matters most?
3. **Diagnostic tree** - How do I investigate?
4. **Trade-offs** - What do I optimize for?
5. **Business impact** - Why does this matter?

---

## 📞 During Interview

**Remember:**
- Think out loud (let them see your process)
- Listen actively (adjust based on hints)
- Be conversational (not a lecture)
- Use frameworks (but don't be robotic)
- Connect the dots (don't leave things isolated)
- Ask for feedback ("Does this direction make sense?")

---

## 🏆 What Interviewers Love

✅ "Before I dive in, can I ask a couple clarifying questions?"  
✅ "Let me think about this from multiple angles..."  
✅ "That's a good point. I hadn't considered that."  
✅ "Here's a potential trade-off..."  
✅ "Let me walk through my thinking step by step..."  
✅ "Does this approach make sense to you?"

## 🚀 After the Interview

**Reflect:**
1. Which questions did I struggle with?
2. Did I ask clarifying questions?
3. Did I connect back to business impact?
4. Did I discuss trade-offs?
5. Was I conversational or lecturing?

**Practice:**
1. Explain metrics in plain English
2. Draw diagrams showing relationships
3. Discuss competing priorities
4. Pivot when given new info
5. Connect everything back to strategy
