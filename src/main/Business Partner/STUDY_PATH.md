## 📊 Practice Scenarios (Do These!)

### **Scenario 1: Measurement Question**
```
Interviewer: "How would you measure the success of DashPass (our subscription product)?"

Your approach:
1. Clarify: Who's the audience? What's success?
2. Define: Primary, secondary, guardrail metrics
3. Data: What sources would you pull?
4. Dashboard: How would you visualize?
5. Impact: Why does this matter?

Time: 45 minutes
Reference: BP5_Feature_Product_Launch_Success.md + BP1_Master_Guide.md
```

---

### **Scenario 2: Investigation Question**
```
Interviewer: "D30 retention dropped from 45% to 40% this month. Investigate."

Your approach:
1. Validate: Is this real? How sudden?
2. Segment: Which cohorts affected?
3. Root cause: Demand/supply/quality/ops?
4. Diagnosis: What specifically changed?
5. Actions: What would you do?

Time: 45 minutes
Reference: BP9_Sales_Order_Drop_Investigation.md + BP1_Master_Guide.md
```

---

### **Scenario 3: Strategy Question**
```
Interviewer: "We're launching in Canada next quarter. How do we approach this?"

Your approach:
1. Clarify: Market goals, constraints?
2. Success: Define go/no-go criteria
3. Metrics: What KPIs matter?
4. Phasing: Soft launch → scale approach
5. Implementation: Timeline and next steps

Time: 45 minutes
Reference: BP4_New_Market_Vertical_Expansion.md + BP1_Master_Guide.md
```

---

### **Scenario 4: Vague Ask**
```
Interviewer: "Build a model to predict which users will churn."

Your approach:
1. Clarify: What's the business problem?
2. Success: How will we measure?
3. Data: What exists? Quality?
4. Approach: Data → features → model
5. MVP: What would we build first?

Time: 45 minutes
Reference: BP6_Vague_Model_Requirements.md + BP1_Master_Guide.md
```

---

### **Scenario 5: Experiment Question**
```
Interviewer: "Design an experiment to test if lower prices increase repeat orders."

Your approach:
1. Hypothesis: Clear statement
2. Metrics: Primary, secondary, guardrails
3. Sample: How many users? How long?
4. Design: How do we randomize?
5. Decision: How do we interpret results?

Time: 45 minutes
Reference: BP8_Experimentation_AB_Testing.md + BP1_Master_Guide.md
```

## 🎤 Key Phrases to Practice

**When you don't know something:**
- "That's a great point. I hadn't considered that."
- "I don't have data on that, but here's how I'd find out."
- "Let me think through that differently..."

**When clarifying:**
- "Before I dive in, can I ask a few clarifying questions?"
- "Help me understand the context better..."
- "Who's the primary user of this analysis?"

**When thinking:**
- "Let me walk through my thinking step by step..."
- "I'd approach this by first understanding..."
- "There are a few ways to look at this..."

**When wrapping up:**
- "To summarize, I'd focus on..."
- "Does this direction make sense to you?"
- "What aspects would you like me to go deeper on?"

## 🎁 Pro Tips

**The Secret Sauce:**
Most candidates skip clarifying questions. You won't. This alone sets you apart.

**The Timing Hack:**
Ask clarifying questions in the first 5 minutes. Spend 40 minutes on solution. Don't rush into details.

**The Listening Hack:**
Pay attention to hints in the interviewer's questions. They often tell you what they care about.

**The Communication Hack:**
Explain like you're talking to someone who isn't in your field. Simple > Complex.

**The Confidence Hack:**
"I don't know, but here's how I'd find out" is better than guessing.
